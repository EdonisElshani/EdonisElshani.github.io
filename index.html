Edonis Elshani

Robotics, Optimization, and Autonomous Systems

I am a Master's student at the Technical University of Munich and a visiting research fellow at Harvard University. My work focuses on geometric mechanics, certifiable trajectory optimization, and robotics.

I am especially interested in combining mathematically rigorous modeling with practical robotic systems, from autonomous driving and localization to trajectory optimization on Lie groups.

About

I am currently working on my Master's thesis at the Computational Robotics Group at Harvard University, where I develop Lie group variational integrators and semidefinite relaxations for trajectory optimization of robotic systems.

Before that, I worked at BMW on Level 3 autonomous driving and at Knorr-Bremse on sensor validation and integration for rail applications.

Current interests
  • Geometric mechanics and Lie group methods
  • Trajectory optimization and certifiable optimization
  • Autonomous systems and robotic decision-making
  • Localization, mapping, and navigation in ROS2
